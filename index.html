TREATY 4 GATHERING

Treaty 4 was signed on September 15, 1874, with people from British Queen Victoria joining with the Cree, Assiniboine, and Saulteaux peoples. This process took six days to complete.

    • Treaty 4 Location and Coverage

    Treaty 4 covers a large area of southern Saskatchewan, extending into western Manitoba and a small part of Alberta. It includes over 195,000 square kilometers of land. The treaty was signed at Fort Qu'Appelle, Saskatchewan, which remains an important historical site today.

    Areas Included in Treaty 4

    • Southern Saskatchewan (including Regina, Moose Jaw, Yorkton, and Fort Qu'Appelle)
    • Western Manitoba (bordering Saskatchewan)
    • A small section of Alberta

    First Nations in Treaty 4 Territory

    Treaty 4 includes 35 First Nations from the Cree, Saulteaux (Ojibwe), and Assiniboine peoples. Some of these nations are:

    • Cowessess First Nation
    • Piapot First Nation
    • Ochapowace First Nation
    • Pasqua First Nation
    • Kahkewistahaw First Nation

    Why is the Location Important?

    The lands within Treaty 4 were originally home to Indigenous peoples who lived off the land, relying on the natural environment for food, shelter, and trade. The signing of the treaty changed how land was used and led to the creation of reserves. Today, many communities in this area continue to honor Treaty 4 through education, cultural events, and land acknowledgments.
