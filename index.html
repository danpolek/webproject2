What is Treaty 4?

Treaty 4, also known as the Qu'Appelle Treaty, was signed on September 15, 1874 between representatives of the British Crown and Indigenous nations including the Cree, Saulteaux (Ojibwe), and Assiniboine (Nakoda) peoples. The signing took place at Fort Qu'Appelle in what is now Saskatchewan, with over 5,000 Indigenous people attending the negotiations. (Source: The Canadian Encyclopedia)

The treaty established agreements about land use, education, hunting rights, and annual payments in exchange for sharing the territory. The iconic Treaty 4 flag symbolizes this agreement through its four quadrants representing different aspects of the treaty relationship. (Source: File Hills Qu'Appelle Tribal Council)

Treaty 4 Timeline

September 1874

Initial negotiations begin at Fort Qu'Appelle between government representatives and Indigenous leaders.

September 15, 1874

Treaty 4 is formally signed by representatives of the Crown and Indigenous nations.

1875-1877

Adhesions to the treaty are signed by additional First Nations groups.

Present Day

Treaty 4 continues to shape relationships between Indigenous peoples and the Canadian government.

Treaty 4 Territory

Treaty 4 covers approximately 195,000 square kilometers of what is now southern Saskatchewan, parts of western Manitoba and eastern Alberta. The territory includes major cities like Regina, Moose Jaw, and Yorkton. (Source: Indigenous Services Canada)

First Nations of Treaty 4

Cowessess First Nation

Located near Regina, known for their rich cultural heritage.

Piapot First Nation

Named after Chief Piapot who signed the treaty.

Ochapowace Nation

Meaning "the place where the lightning strikes."

Kahkewistahaw First Nation

Meaning "he who flies around" in Cree.

There are 35 First Nations in total under Treaty 4, each with their own unique history and relationship to the treaty. (Source: Treaty 4 Education Alliance)

Treaty 4 Today

Treaty 4 continues to have significant impacts today:

  • Land use agreements and resource development
  • Education and language preservation
  • Economic partnerships and opportunities
  • Cultural revitalization efforts

Many communities hold annual Treaty 4 gatherings to celebrate and honor the agreement. (Source: Treaty 4 Gathering)
