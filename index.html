TREATY 4 GATHERING

Treaty 4 was signed on September 15, 1874, with British Queen Victoria joining with the Cree, Assiniboine, and Saulteaux peoples.

The treaty was signed at Fort Qu'Appelle, Saskatchewan, which remains an important historical site today.

Treaty 4 Location and Coverage

Treaty 4 covers a large area of south Saskatchewan, extending into west Manitoba and part of Alberta, including over 195,000 km².

The Canadian Encyclopedia - Treaty 4

Areas Included in Treaty 4

  • Southern Saskatchewan (e.g. Regina, Moose Jaw, Yorkton)
  • Western Manitoba
  • Part of Alberta

First Nations in Treaty 4 Territory

Treaty 4 includes 35 First Nations including:

  • Cowessess First Nation
  • Piapot First Nation
  • Ochapowace First Nation
  • Pasqua First Nation
  • Kahkewistahaw First Nation

Why is the Location Important?

The land in Treaty 4 was home to Indigenous people who lived off the land, hunted, and traded. The treaty changed land use and led to the creation of reserves.

How Treaty 4 Affects People Today

Treaty 4 continues to impact Indigenous rights and government relations in Canada. It’s honored in schools, events, and land acknowledgments.
